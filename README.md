# Charge Distribution on Disordered Conductor Surfaces

## Overview

This repository accompanies our published research paper, *"A Geometrical Description of Charge Distribution on the Disordered Conductor Surfaces."*

The work investigates the relationship between conductor geometry and surface charge distribution through qualitative electrostatic analysis. It revisits the common assumption that electric charge always accumulates at regions with the highest curvature and presents geometrical examples demonstrating that curvature alone is insufficient to fully explain charge density on conductor surfaces. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

---

## Research Motivation

The distribution of electric charge on conductor surfaces is a fundamental topic in electrostatics and is commonly introduced in undergraduate physics courses. Traditional explanations often associate higher surface charge density with regions of greater curvature.

This study examines several conductor geometries and argues that a more comprehensive geometrical interpretation is required to understand charge accumulation. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

---

## Main Contributions

- Investigates the relationship between conductor geometry and surface charge density.
- Demonstrates that curvature alone cannot completely determine charge distribution.
- Discusses charge behavior on folded conducting surfaces.
- Compares convex and concave surface regions.
- Presents qualitative analysis of knotted torus geometries.
- Provides an educational perspective on electrostatic charge distribution. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
